Journey of Nelson Mandela along with his great works:

  • 1918: Nelson Rohlilahla Mandela (byname) Madiba was born on july 18 , 1918 in South Africa.
  • 1938: He attended South African Native College (later the University of Fort Hare).
  • 1941: He studied law at the University of the Witwatersrand; he later passed the qualification exam to become a lawyer.
  • 1944: He joined the African National Congress (ANC), a Black-liberation group, and became a leader of its Youth League.
  • 1944-1948: He subsequently held other ANC leadership positions, through which he helped revitalize the organization and oppose the apartheid policies of the ruling National Party.
  • 1952: In Johannesburg, with fellow ANC leader Oliver Tambo, Mandela established South Africa’s first Black law practice, specializing in cases resulting from the post-1948 apartheid legislation.
  • 1952: He played an important role in launching a campaign of defiance against South Africa’s pass laws, which required nonwhites to carry documents (known as passes, pass books, or reference books) authorizing their presence in areas that the government deemed “restricted” (i.e., generally reserved for the white population).
  • 1955: He was involved in drafting the Freedom Charter, a document calling for nonracial social democracy in South Africa.
  • 1956: He was arrested with more than 100 other people on charges of treason that were designed to harass antiapartheid activists.
  • 1962: He went to Algeria for training in guerrilla warfare and sabotage, returning to South Africa later that year.
  • 1964: He was sentenced to life imprisonment, narrowly escaping the death penalty.
  • 1970-1988: He was imprisonated for protesting against racism.
  • 1990: The South African government under President de Klerk released Mandela from prison.
  • 1991: Mandela was chosen deputy president of the ANC; he became president of the party in July 1991.
  • 1992: Mandela led the ANC in negotiations with de Klerk to end apartheid and bring about a peaceful transition to nonracial democracy in South Africa.
  • 2007: He was a founding member of the Elders, a group of international leaders established in 2007.
  • 2008: Mandela was feted with several celebrations in South Africa, Great Britain, and other countries in honour of his 90th birthday.
  • 2009: Mandela Day, observed on Mandela’s birthday, was created to honour his legacy by promoting community service around the world. It was first observed on July 18, 2009
  • 2013: Nelson Mandela died in the year 2013.
Mandela’s writings and speeches were collected in I Am Prepared to Die (1964; rev. ed. 1986), No Easy Walk to Freedom (1965; updated ed. 2002), The Struggle Is My Life (1978; rev. ed. 1990), and In His Own Words (2003). The autobiography Long Walk to Freedom, which chronicles his early life and years in prison, was published in 1994. An unfinished draft of his second volume of memoirs was completed by Mandla Langa and released posthumously as Dare Not Linger: The Presidential Years (2017).
